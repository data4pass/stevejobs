Steve Jobs

The Pioneer of the Microcomputer Revolution

"Of all the inventions of humans, the computer is going to rank near or at the top as history unfolds and we look back. It is the most awesome tool that we have ever invented. I feel incredibly lucky to be at exactly the right place in Silicon Valley, at exactly the right time, historically, where this invention has taken form."

Brief Recount of Steve Jobs's Life

1955 - Born as Steven Paul Jobs in San Fransisco.

1969 - Met Steve Wozniak, who shares a lot of common interests with Steve.

1972 - Together with Wozniak, they built and illegally sold 'blue boxes' that allow to make phone calls for free.

1974 - Got his first job at Atari, later made a trip to India where he took an interest in Zen Buddhism.

Mar 1976 - Together with Wozniak, they shown the early Apple I board at the Homebrew Computer Club.

Apr 1976 - Apple Computer Inc. was incorporated by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne.

1977 - Former Intel executive turned business angel Mike Markkula invested in Apple and hired former colleague Mike Scott as CEO. Woz was forced to leave HP to join Apple full time.

1985 - After a complex power struggle in Apple, Jobs resigned from Apple and founded NeXT Inc.

1989 - Making use of a NeXT computer, English computer scientist Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web in 1989 at CERN in Switzerland.

1986 - Funded the spinout of The Graphics Group (later renamed Pixar).

1997 - NeXT Inc. was bought by Apple Inc. Bringing Jobs back to the company he had cofounded. He later became the de facto chief of the company.

2003 - Diagnosed with pancreatic cancer.

2011 - Died of respiratory arrest related to his pancreatic tumor, he was 56 years old.
